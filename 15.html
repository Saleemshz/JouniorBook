Deosai Plains

Pakistan is home to the world's second highest plateau in the Deosai National Park in Gilgit-Baltistan. Known as "Land of Gaints", the Deosai Plateau is a high-altitude alpine plain situated at an elevation of 13,497 ft above sea level. Each spring, the lush valley is swept by wildflowers and rare butterflies, earning the name "Summer's Place". The plains are home to a wide array of rare flora and fauna including the Himalayan Brown Bear.
